Profit & Loss – Quantitative Aptitude

Introduction

Profit and Loss are crucial topics in the Quantitative Aptitude sections of various competitive and placement exams. A strong understanding of Cost Price (CP), Selling Price (SP), discounts, and related formulas is essential to solve these questions quickly and accurately.

Profit and Loss – Basic Concepts

Profit: Extra money earned when Selling Price (SP) is more than Cost Price (CP).
Loss: Money lost when Selling Price (SP) is less than Cost Price (CP).
Cost Price (CP): The amount paid to purchase an item.
Selling Price (SP): The amount received by selling an item.
Marked Price (MP): Original listed price before discount.
Discount: Reduction applied on the marked price.

Profit and Loss Formulas

Name Formula
Profit Profit = SP − CP (SP > CP)
Loss Loss = CP − SP (CP > SP)
Profit Percentage Profit% = (Profit / CP) × 100
Loss Percentage Loss% = (Loss / CP) × 100
Selling Price (Profit) SP = [(100 + Profit%) / 100] × CP
Selling Price (Loss) SP = [(100 − Loss%) / 100] × CP
Cost Price (Profit) CP = [100 / (100 + Profit%)] × SP
Cost Price (Loss) CP = [100 / (100 − Loss%)] × SP
Discount Discount = MP − SP

Shortcut Method for Profit & Loss

Write the ratio of CP : SP and reduce it to the lowest form a : b.

Profit% = ((b − a) / a) × 100

This method helps calculate profit or loss quickly without lengthy steps.

Solved Questions

Q1. A person buys 30 notebooks for Rs.12 and sells 20 notebooks for Rs.15.
Answer: Profit = 87.5%
Q2. Two successive discounts of 15% and 25% and a member discount of 10% are given.
Answer: Overall discount = 42.63%
Q3. CP of a pen is Rs.10 for 20 pens and SP is Rs.10 for 15 pens.
Answer: Profit = 33.33%
Q4. A loss of 5% at SP = Rs.1805. Find SP for 5% gain.
Answer: Rs.1995
Q5. CP is 67% of SP. Find profit percent.
Answer: 49.25%
