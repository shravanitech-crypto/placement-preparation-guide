English Grammar – Complete Verbal Ability

Introduction

English grammar is the foundation of placement verbal ability. Companies test grammar through error detection, sentence correction, voice change, speech change, and fill-in-the-blanks.

Parts of Speech

  • Noun: Person, place, thing, idea
  • Pronoun: Replaces noun
  • Verb: Action or state
  • Adjective: Describes noun
  • Adverb: Describes verb/adjective
  • Preposition: Shows relation
  • Conjunction: Joins words
  • Interjection: Expresses emotion

All 12 Tenses (Important)

Present: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous Past: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous Future: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous

Most asked: Present Perfect, Past Perfect, Future Tense usage.

Subject-Verb Agreement

  • Each/Every → Singular verb
  • Everyone/Someone → Singular
  • A number of → Plural
  • The number of → Singular
  • Either/Neither → Singular

Articles

  • a → before consonant sound
  • an → before vowel sound
  • the → specific object

Important Prepositions

  • Good at
  • Interested in
  • Afraid of
  • Fond of
  • Capable of

Conjunctions

  • And, But, Or
  • Although, Because, Since
  • Either-Or, Neither-Nor

Active & Passive Voice

Active: Ram wrote the letter. Passive: The letter was written by Ram.

Direct & Indirect Speech

Direct: He said, "I am ready." Indirect: He said that he was ready.

Types of Sentences

  • Simple
  • Compound
  • Complex
  • Compound-Complex

Punctuation

  • Comma (,)
  • Full Stop (.)
  • Semicolon (;)
  • Colon (:)
  • Apostrophe (’)
