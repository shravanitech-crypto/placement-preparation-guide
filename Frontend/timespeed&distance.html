Time, Speed & Distance – Quantitative Aptitude

Introduction

Time, Speed and Distance form the foundation of Engineering Mechanics, Physics, Transportation Engineering and Competitive Aptitude Exams. Mastery of this topic is essential for GATE, PSU, SSC, Banking and Placement exams.

Basic Formulas

Speed = Distance / Time
Distance = Speed × Time
Time = Distance / Speed

Types of Speed

Uniform Speed: Constant speed throughout motion.
Non-Uniform Speed: Speed changes with time.
Instantaneous Speed: v = ds/dt (Used in Engineering Calculus).
Average Speed: Total Distance / Total Time

Relative Speed

  • Opposite Direction → S1 + S2
  • Same Direction → |S1 - S2|

Train Formulas

  • Crossing Pole → Speed = Length / Time
  • Crossing Platform → Speed = (Train + Platform) / Time
  • Two Trains Opposite → (L1 + L2) / (S1 + S2)
  • Two Trains Same → (L1 + L2) / (S1 - S2)

Boats & Streams

  • Downstream = u + v
  • Upstream = u - v
  • u = (Down + Up) / 2
  • v = (Down - Up) / 2

Engineering Concepts

  • Speed-Time Graph → Area gives Distance
  • Acceleration → v = u + at
  • s = ut + ½at²
  • Dimension of Speed = [L T⁻¹]
