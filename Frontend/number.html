Numbers – Quantitative Aptitude

Introduction

Numbers are the fundamental units of mathematics. A strong understanding of numbers is essential for solving aptitude questions efficiently. Topics such as divisibility, remainders, cyclicity, HCF & LCM are frequently asked in placement exams.

Types of Numbers

Integers: Numbers with no fractional part
Examples: -3, -2, -1, 0, 5, 10
Natural Numbers: Counting numbers starting from 1
Examples: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
Whole Numbers: Natural numbers including 0
Examples: 0, 1, 2, 3
Prime Numbers: Numbers having exactly two factors
Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 53
Composite Numbers: Numbers greater than 1 that are not prime
Examples: 4, 60, 91

Important Points on Prime Numbers

  • 0 and 1 are neither prime nor composite
  • 2 is the only even prime number
  • There are 25 prime numbers less than 100
  • Two numbers are co-prime if their HCF is 1

Number of Divisors

If a number is written in prime factorized form as p₁ᵉ¹ × p₂ᵉ² × p₃ᵉ³, then number of divisors = (e₁+1)(e₂+1)(e₃+1)

Example: 200 = 2³ × 5²
Divisors = (3+1)(2+1) = 12

Divisibility Rules

  • 2: Last digit even
  • 3: Sum of digits divisible by 3
  • 4: Last two digits divisible by 4
  • 5: Last digit 0 or 5
  • 6: Divisible by both 2 and 3
  • 7: Double last digit and subtract
  • 8: Last three digits divisible by 8
  • 9: Sum of digits divisible by 9
  • 11: Difference of odd & even place digits multiple of 11

Remainder Rules

  • Divide by 2 → check last digit
  • Divide by 3 or 9 → sum of digits
  • Divide by 4 → last two digits
  • Divide by 8 → last three digits

Cyclicity of Numbers

Units digit repeats in a fixed cycle when powers increase.

  • 2 → 2, 4, 8, 6
  • 3 → 3, 9, 7, 1
  • 7 → 7, 9, 3, 1
  • 8 → 8, 4, 2, 6

Solved Questions

Q: How many 4-digit numbers are divisible by 7?
A: 1286
Q: Find the unit digit of (17)¹⁵³ × (31)⁶²
A: 7
