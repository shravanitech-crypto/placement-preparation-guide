Time and Work – Quantitative Aptitude

Introduction

Time and Work is one of the most basic concepts in Quantitative Aptitude, which is tested extensively in government exams. Undoubtedly, having a strong background in this topic can give candidates an upper hand and help them to score well in competitive exams. A candidate needs to be aware of the basic concept and the types of questions that may be asked in the exams, mainly Banking and SSC exams.

The following article consists of question patterns and formulas that will be useful to the candidates while preparing for the examination. Try and practice the questions given below for a better understanding of the topic.

Question Patterns on Time and Work

  • To find the efficiency of an individual or a group
  • To find the time taken by a person or a group to do a piece of work or a job
  • A certain work done by an individual in a certain period
  • A certain work done by a group of individuals in a certain period
  • To find wages

Time and Work Formulas

Work Done by a person = Time Taken × Rate of Work
Rate of Work of a person = 1 / Time Taken by him
Time Taken by him = 1 / Rate of Work
If a piece of work or a job is done in n number of days, then the work done in one day = 1/n
Total Work Done = Number of Days × Efficiency
Efficiency of work done and Time are inversely proportional to each other.
M : W is the ratio of the number of men and women which are required to complete a piece of work, then the ratio of the time taken by them to complete the work will be W : M.
(M1 × D1 × T1 × W2) = (M2 × D2 × T2 × W1)

Solved Examples on Time and Work

Q1. L can finish a work in 16 days and M can do the same work in 12 days. With help of N, they did the work in 4 days only. Then, N alone can do the work in how many days?
  • A. 48/5 days
  • B. 48/7 days
  • C. 48/11 days
  • D. 10 days
  • E. None of these
Answer: A
Solution:

(L + M + N)'s 1 day's work = 1/4

L's 1 day's work = 1/16

M's 1 day's work = 1/12

Therefore, N's 1 day's work
= 1/4 − (1/16 + 1/12)
= 1/4 − 7/48 = 5/48

So, N alone can do the work in 48/5 days.
Q2. A man can do a work in 20 days and a woman in 15 days. If they work on it together for 5 days, then the fraction of the work that is left is:
  • A. 1/12
  • B. 1/10
  • C. 5/12
  • D. 7/15
  • E. 8/15
Answer: C
Solution:

Man's 1 day's work = 1/20

Woman's 1 day's work = 1/15

(Man + woman)'s 1 day's work = (1/20 + 1/15) = 7/60

(Man + woman)'s 5 day's work = (7/60 × 5) = 7/12

Thus, Remaining work = 1 − 7/12 = 5/12
Q3. P, Q and R can do a job in 20, 30 and 60 days respectively. In how many days can P do the job if he is assisted by Q and R every third day?
  • A. 11 days
  • B. 15 days
  • C. 17 days
  • D. 16 days
  • E. 18 days
Answer: B
Solution:

P's 2 day's work = 2/20 = 1/10

(P + Q + R)'s 1 day's work
= (1/20 + 1/30 + 1/60)
= 6/60 = 1/10

Job done in 3 days = (1/10 + 1/10) = 1/5

Now, 1/5 job is done in 3 days
Whole job will be done in (3 × 5) = 15 days.
Q4. M's efficiency is three times N's efficiency. M can finish a job in 60 days less than N. If they work together, then in how many days the job will be done?
  • A. 20 days
  • B. 22.5 days
  • C. 25 days
  • D. 30 days
  • E. 24.5 days
Answer: B
Solution:

Ratio of times taken by M and N = 1 : 3
(Since the efficiency of M is three times N)

Time difference = (3 − 1) = 2 units = 60 days
1 unit = 30 days

M takes 30 days and N takes 90 days

M's 1 day's work = 1/30
N's 1 day's work = 1/90

(M + N)'s 1 day's work = (1/30 + 1/90) = 2/45

Time taken together = 45/2 = 22.5 days
Q5. Ankit alone can do a piece of work in 6 days and Bishal alone in 8 days. Ankit and Bishal undertook to do it for Rs. 4800. With the help of Dinesh, they completed the work in 3 days. How much is to be paid to Dinesh?
  • A. Rs. 1375
  • B. Rs. 1400
  • C. Rs. 1600
  • D. Rs. 600
  • E. Rs. 2000
Answer: D
Solution:

Ankit's 1 day work = 1/6
Bishal's 1 day work = 1/8

(Ankit + Bishal + Dinesh)'s 1 day work = 1/3

Dinesh's 1 day work = 1/3 − (1/6 + 1/8) = 1/24

Dinesh's 3 day work = 3 × 1/24 = 1/8

Payment to Dinesh = 4800 × 1/8 = Rs. 600
